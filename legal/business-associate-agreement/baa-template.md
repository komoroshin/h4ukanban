Designated Record Set shall have the meaning given to such term under 45 CFR § 164.501.

### 1.7 **HIPAA Rules**
HIPAA Rules shall mean the federal regulations implementing HIPAA, including the Privacy Rule (45 CFR Part 160 and Part 164, Subparts A and E), the Security Rule (45 CFR Part 160 and Part 164, Subparts A and C), the Breach Notification Rule (45 CFR Part 164, Subpart D), and other applicable provisions of 45 CFR Parts 160, 162, and 164, all as amended from time to time.

## ARTICLE 2: PERMITTED AND REQUIRED USES AND DISCLOSURES OF PHI

### 2.1 **Permitted Uses and Disclosures**

Business Associate may use or disclose Protected Health Information only as permitted or required by this Agreement, as Required by Law, or as otherwise permitted by the HIPAA Rules. Specifically, Business Associate may:

#### 2.1.1 **Services to Covered Entity**
Use or disclose Protected Health Information as necessary to perform the Services for Covered Entity as specified in the Underlying Agreement and Healthy4U Terms of Service, provided such use or disclosure would not violate the Privacy Rule if done by Covered Entity.

#### 2.1.2 **Business Associate's Management and Administration**
Use Protected Health Information for the proper management and administration of Business Associate or to carry out the legal responsibilities of Business Associate, provided:
- The use is necessary for such management, administration, or legal responsibilities; AND
- Business Associate obtains reasonable assurances from the person to whom the information is disclosed that:
  - The information will remain confidential and will be used or further disclosed only as Required by Law or for the purpose for which it was disclosed to the person; AND
  - The person will notify Business Associate of any instances of which it is aware in which the confidentiality of the information has been breached.

#### 2.1.3 **Data Aggregation**
Use Protected Health Information to provide Data Aggregation services to Covered Entity as permitted by 45 CFR § 164.504(e)(2)(i)(B), if applicable.

#### 2.1.4 **De-Identification**
Use Protected Health Information to create de-identified data in accordance with 45 CFR § 164.514(a)-(c), provided that such de-identified data may be used and disclosed by Business Associate for any purpose. Business Associate shall not attempt to re-identify de-identified data or contact individuals whose data has been de-identified.

### 2.2 **Prohibited Uses and Disclosures**

#### 2.2.1 **Sale of PHI**
Business Associate shall NOT receive remuneration, directly or indirectly, in exchange for Protected Health Information, except as permitted by 45 CFR § 164.502(a)(5)(ii).

#### 2.2.2 **Marketing**
Business Associate shall NOT use or disclose Protected Health Information for marketing purposes, except as permitted by 45 CFR § 164.508(a)(3) or as otherwise authorized by Covered Entity.

#### 2.2.3 **Fundraising**
Business Associate shall NOT use or disclose Protected Health Information for fundraising purposes without Covered Entity's prior written authorization.

### 2.3 **Required Disclosures**

Business Associate shall disclose Protected Health Information:

#### 2.3.1 **To Individuals**
To an Individual when required under 45 CFR § 164.524 (Right of Access) or 45 CFR § 164.528 (Right to an Accounting of Disclosures), as applicable and as directed by Covered Entity.

#### 2.3.2 **To HHS**
To the Secretary of the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services for purposes of determining Covered Entity's or Business Associate's compliance with the HIPAA Rules.

### 2.4 **Minimum Necessary**

Business Associate shall make reasonable efforts to limit uses, disclosures, and requests for Protected Health Information to the minimum necessary to accomplish the intended purpose, as required by 45 CFR § 164.502(b), except where the HIPAA Rules provide an exception.

## ARTICLE 3: OBLIGATIONS OF BUSINESS ASSOCIATE

### 3.1 **Safeguards**

Business Associate shall implement and maintain appropriate administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to prevent use or disclosure of Protected Health Information other than as provided for by this Agreement.

Business Associate shall comply with the requirements of the Security Rule (45 CFR Part 164, Subparts A and C) with respect to Electronic Protected Health Information, including but not limited to:

#### 3.1.1 **Administrative Safeguards** (45 CFR § 164.308)
- **Security Management Process**: Implement policies and procedures to prevent, detect, contain, and correct security violations
- **Workforce Security**: Ensure workforce members have appropriate access to ePHI and prevent unauthorized access
- **Information Access Management**: Implement policies and procedures for authorizing access to ePHI
- **Security Awareness and Training**: Train workforce on security policies and procedures
- **Security Incident Procedures**: Identify and respond to suspected or known security incidents
- **Contingency Plan**: Establish policies and procedures for responding to emergencies (data backup, disaster recovery, emergency mode operations)
- **Evaluation**: Periodically evaluate security policies and procedures

#### 3.1.2 **Physical Safeguards** (45 CFR § 164.310)
- **Facility Access Controls**: Limit physical access to facilities while ensuring authorized access
- **Workstation Use**: Implement policies governing use of workstations that access ePHI
- **Workstation Security**: Implement physical safeguards for workstations
- **Device and Media Controls**: Implement policies for disposal, removal, and re-use of electronic media containing ePHI

#### 3.1.3 **Technical Safeguards** (45 CFR § 164.312)
- **Access Control**: Implement technical policies and procedures for ePHI systems that allow access only to authorized persons
  - **Encryption**: Business Associate shall encrypt all ePHI at rest using AES-256 encryption and ePHI in transit using TLS 1.3 or higher
  - **Unique User Identification**: Assign unique identifiers for users accessing ePHI
  - **Automatic Logoff**: Implement procedures to terminate sessions after a predetermined time of inactivity
- **Audit Controls**: Implement hardware, software, and/or procedural mechanisms to record and examine access to ePHI
- **Integrity**: Implement policies to ensure ePHI is not improperly altered or destroyed
- **Transmission Security**: Implement technical security measures to guard against unauthorized access to ePHI transmitted over electronic networks

### 3.2 **Breach Notification**

#### 3.2.1 **Discovery and Investigation**
Business Associate shall, following discovery of a Breach of Unsecured Protected Health Information:
- Conduct an internal investigation to determine the scope of the Breach (what PHI was involved, how many individuals affected, what caused the Breach)
- Document the investigation and findings
- Determine whether the Breach meets the threshold for notification under 45 CFR § 164.404(a)

#### 3.2.2 **Notification to Covered Entity**
Business Associate shall notify Covered Entity of the Breach **no later than sixty (60) calendar days after discovery** of the Breach. Notification shall be made to:

[Covered Entity Notification Contact]
Email: [Email]
Phone: [Phone]
Fax: [Fax]

Notification shall include, to the extent possible:
- Identification of each Individual whose Unsecured Protected Health Information has been, or is reasonably believed to have been, accessed, acquired, used, or disclosed during the Breach
- A brief description of what happened, including the date of the Breach and the date of discovery
- A description of the types of Unsecured Protected Health Information involved in the Breach (e.g., name, Social Security number, date of birth, medical record number, diagnosis, treatment information)
- The number of individuals affected
- Steps Business Associate has taken or will take to mitigate any deleterious effect of the Breach
- Steps Business Associate recommends that Covered Entity take to protect individuals affected
- Contact information for individuals to ask questions or learn more

#### 3.2.3 **Covered Entity's Breach Notification Obligations**
Business Associate acknowledges that, except as provided in 45 CFR § 164.410, following notification of a Breach by Business Associate, Covered Entity remains responsible for:
- Notifying affected individuals without unreasonable delay and in no case later than 60 calendar days after discovery of the Breach (45 CFR § 164.404)
- Notifying the Secretary of HHS (45 CFR § 164.408)
- Notifying prominent media outlets if the Breach affects more than 500 residents of a State or jurisdiction (45 CFR § 164.406)

#### 3.2.4 **Cooperation**
Business Associate shall reasonably cooperate with Covered Entity in meeting Covered Entity's Breach notification obligations, including providing timely information and documentation requested by Covered Entity.

#### 3.2.5 **Ongoing Notification**
If Business Associate cannot identify all individuals affected by a Breach or determine all facts relevant to the Breach by the time of initial notification, Business Associate shall provide updates to Covered Entity as additional information becomes available.

### 3.3 **Security Incidents**

Business Associate shall report to Covered Entity any Security Incident (as defined in 45 CFR § 164.304) of which it becomes aware. Notification shall be provided:
- **Immediately** (within 24 hours) for incidents involving unauthorized access to, or disclosure of, Unsecured Protected Health Information
- **Within 72 hours** for other Security Incidents that do not constitute Breaches

**Ongoing Security Incidents**: The parties acknowledge that certain ongoing security incidents (e.g., port scans, ping sweeps, unsuccessful log-on attempts) are a routine part of system operations and do not require individual notification. Business Associate shall document and report such incidents in periodic security reports (quarterly or as requested by Covered Entity).

#### 3.4.1 **Business Associate Agreements**

### 3.5 **Individual Rights**

Business Associate shall, within ten (10) business days of request by Covered Entity, make available to Covered Entity the information required for Covered Entity to fulfill its obligations to provide access to, accounting of, or amendment of Protected Health Information, as described below:

#### 3.5.1 **Right of Access** (45 CFR § 164.524)
Upon request by Covered Entity, Business Associate shall provide access to Protected Health Information in a Designated Record Set to Covered Entity or, as directed by Covered Entity, to an Individual, to the extent necessary to satisfy Covered Entity's obligations under 45 CFR § 164.524.

Business Associate shall provide such access within **thirty (30) calendar days** of the request (or such shorter timeframe as required to meet Covered Entity's obligations).

Access shall be provided in the form and format requested by the Individual (if readily producible in such form and format) or in a readable hard copy or electronic format as agreed upon by Business Associate and Covered Entity or the Individual.

**Fees**: Business Associate may charge a reasonable, cost-based fee as permitted by 45 CFR § 164.524(c)(4) for providing copies. The fee shall not exceed the actual cost of:
- Labor for copying the PHI
- Supplies for creating and mailing the PHI
- Postage (if the Individual requests mailing)
- Preparing an explanation or summary (if agreed to by the Individual)

#### 3.5.2 **Right to Amend** (45 CFR § 164.526)
Upon request by Covered Entity, Business Associate shall make amendments to Protected Health Information in a Designated Record Set as directed by Covered Entity within **thirty (30) calendar days**.

Business Associate is not required to agree to an amendment but shall comply with Covered Entity's direction to amend if Covered Entity determines an amendment is appropriate.

#### 3.5.3 **Accounting of Disclosures** (45 CFR § 164.528)
Business Associate shall document all disclosures of Protected Health Information and information related to such disclosures as would be required for Covered Entity to respond to a request by an Individual for an accounting of disclosures in accordance with 45 CFR § 164.528.

Upon request by Covered Entity, Business Associate shall provide an accounting of disclosures to Covered Entity or, as directed by Covered Entity, to an Individual within **thirty (30) calendar days**.

The accounting shall include:
- Date of disclosure
- Name and address of entity or person who received the PHI
- Brief description of the PHI disclosed
- Brief statement of purpose of the disclosure

**Exceptions**: The accounting need not include disclosures:
- To carry out treatment, payment, or healthcare operations (if Business Associate is a Business Associate of the Covered Entity)
- To the Individual or the Individual's personal representative
- Pursuant to an Individual's authorization
- For facility directory or to persons involved in the Individual's care
- For national security or intelligence purposes
- To correctional institutions or law enforcement officials
- As part of a limited data set

#### 3.5.4 **Right to Restrict Disclosures** (45 CFR § 164.522)
If Covered Entity restricts the use or disclosure of Protected Health Information pursuant to a request by an Individual under 45 CFR § 164.522(a), Business Associate shall comply with the restriction upon notice from Covered Entity.

#### 3.5.5 **Minimum Necessary**
Business Associate shall make available to Covered Entity the information required to permit Covered Entity to respond to requests for restrictions on disclosures under 45 CFR § 164.522.

### 3.6 **Compliance with HIPAA Rules**

Business Associate shall comply with all applicable requirements of the HIPAA Rules, including but not limited to the Privacy Rule, Security Rule, and Breach Notification Rule, to the extent that Business Associate is subject to such requirements.

Business Associate shall not use or disclose Protected Health Information in a manner that would violate the HIPAA Rules if done by Covered Entity, except as permitted by this Agreement for Business Associate's management and administration or to carry out its legal responsibilities.

### 3.7 **Availability of Books and Records**

Business Associate shall make its internal practices, books, and records relating to the use and disclosure of Protected Health Information received from, or created or received by Business Associate on behalf of, Covered Entity available to the Secretary for purposes of determining compliance with the HIPAA Rules.

This obligation shall survive termination of this Agreement.

### 3.8 **Mitigation**

Business Associate shall mitigate, to the extent practicable, any harmful effect that is known to Business Associate of a use or disclosure of Protected Health Information by Business Associate in violation of the requirements of this Agreement.

Mitigation steps may include, but are not limited to:
- Notification to affected individuals
- Credit monitoring or identity theft protection services (if Social Security numbers or financial information affected)
- Retrieval of improperly disclosed PHI
- Additional training for workforce members

## ARTICLE 5: TERM AND TERMINATION

### 5.1 **Term**

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in effect until terminated in accordance with this Article 5, or until all of the Protected Health Information provided by Covered Entity to Business Associate, or created or received by Business Associate on behalf of Covered Entity, is destroyed or returned to Covered Entity, whichever is sooner.

### 5.2 **Termination for Cause**

#### 5.2.1 **By Covered Entity**
Covered Entity may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice to Business Associate if Covered Entity determines that Business Associate has:
- Breached a material term of this Agreement and has not cured the breach within thirty (30) calendar days of receiving written notice of the breach; OR
- Breached a material term where cure is not possible.

Upon termination for cause, Covered Entity may, at its option:
- Report the breach to the Secretary; and/or
- Terminate the Underlying Agreement.

#### 5.2.2 **By Business Associate**
Business Associate may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice to Covered Entity if Covered Entity has breached a material term of this Agreement and has not cured the breach within thirty (30) calendar days of receiving written notice of the breach.

### 5.3 **Effect of Termination**

#### 5.3.1 **Return or Destruction of PHI**
Upon termination of this Agreement for any reason, Business Associate shall, at the option of Covered Entity:

**Option A: Return**
Return to Covered Entity all Protected Health Information received from Covered Entity, or created or received by Business Associate on behalf of Covered Entity, that Business Associate maintains in any form. Business Associate shall not retain any copies.

**Option B: Destruction**
Destroy all Protected Health Information received from Covered Entity, or created or received by Business Associate on behalf of Covered Entity, that Business Associate maintains in any form. Business Associate shall provide Covered Entity with certification of destruction.

**Timeline**: Return or destruction shall be completed within **ninety (90) calendar days** of termination.

**Form of Return**: PHI shall be returned in an electronic format (FHIR-compliant JSON, HL7, or CSV) on encrypted media or via secure transmission.

#### 5.3.2 **If Return or Destruction Not Feasible**
If Business Associate determines that returning or destroying Protected Health Information is not feasible (e.g., PHI is commingled with other data, destruction would violate law, or Technical limitations prevent extraction), Business Associate shall:

- Notify Covered Entity in writing within thirty (30) calendar days of termination, explaining why return or destruction is not feasible;
- Extend the protections of this Agreement to such Protected Health Information; and
- Limit further uses and disclosures of such Protected Health Information to those purposes that make the return or destruction infeasible, for so long as Business Associate maintains such Protected Health Information.

#### 5.3.3 **Subcontractors**
Business Associate shall ensure that all Subcontractors return or destroy Protected Health Information in accordance with this Section 5.3, or provide Covered Entity with written confirmation that such return or destruction is not feasible.

### 5.4 **Survival**

The obligations of Business Associate under this Article 5 and Sections 3.7 (Availability of Books and Records) and 6.4 (Indemnification, if applicable) shall survive termination of this Agreement.
