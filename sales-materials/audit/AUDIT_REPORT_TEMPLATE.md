## 6.4 Risk Mitigation

### Risk #1: Physician Resistance

**Risk:** Some physicians may resist new technology, prefer status quo.

**Mitigation:**
- ‚úÖ Pilot with champions first (demonstrate value before asking skeptics)
- ‚úÖ Show data (time savings, satisfaction from pilot)
- ‚úÖ Make adoption voluntary initially (peer pressure works better than mandates)
- ‚úÖ Address concerns (accuracy, ease of use) with live demos and trial

**Contingency:** If a physician truly refuses, don't force. Focus on willing adopters first.

### Risk #2: Low Patient Adoption (App)

**Risk:** Patients don't download or use the app.

**Mitigation:**
- ‚úÖ Multi-channel onboarding (email, SMS, in-office signage, physician recommendation)
- ‚úÖ Incentivize (e.g., "$5 off copay if you sign up during visit")
- ‚úÖ Keep Free tier valuable (so patients don't need to pay to use)
- ‚úÖ Physician champions promote app during visits

**Contingency:** Even if patient adoption is low (30-40% vs 50% target), physician side (AI scribe) still delivers ROI.

### Risk #3: ROI Lower Than Expected

**Risk:** Time savings don't materialize, savings overestimated.

**Mitigation:**
- ‚úÖ Conservative projections (50% time savings, not 75%)
- ‚úÖ Pilot first (validate assumptions before full rollout)
- ‚úÖ Track metrics religiously (time saved, satisfaction)

**Contingency:** Even in conservative scenario (25% time savings), ROI is still positive. If ROI truly doesn't materialize, can cancel after pilot (low commitment risk).

### Risk #4: Technical Issues (EHR Integration)

**Risk:** EHR integration fails, workflow disrupted.

**Mitigation:**
- ‚úÖ Start with manual workflow (copy-paste SOAP notes into EHR) ‚Äî works even without API
- ‚úÖ Test integrations thoroughly during pilot
- ‚úÖ Healthy4U support available for troubleshooting

**Contingency:** Manual workflow is slower but still saves time vs current state. Can improve integration over time.
